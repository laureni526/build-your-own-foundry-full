# The Solo Path — Growing Your Foundry After the Workshop

*Build Your Own Foundry — self-paced continuation*

> You left the workshop with five small things: one question, a few
> story cards, one hunch, an Open Questions page, and a weekly habit.
>
> Those weren't the product. The habit was.
>
> This path is what the habit grows into.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# How This Path Works

There are no deadlines here. No weeks, no modules, no falling behind.

This path uses the same rule your Foundry does: **you advance on
evidence, not on time.** Each stage ends with a gate — a plain
description of what your system looks like when it's ready for the
next stage. When your evidence matches the gate, walk through. If it
doesn't yet, the answer is the one you already know:

> **Not yet. Keep observing.**

Some people reach Stage 2 in six weeks. Some take six months. Both are
doing it right, because the gate isn't measuring you — it's measuring
your evidence. A slow Foundry built on real stories will outlast a
fast one built on enthusiasm every time.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# The Path at a Glance

| Stage | What grows | What it becomes | The gate (you advance when...) |
|---|---|---|---|
| **1. Feed the System** | Stories, one a week | Your Story Library | 10–15 stories from more than one part of your life; your original hunch confirmed, refuted, or replaced by evidence |
| **2. Name Your Patterns** | Hunches into named patterns | Your Pattern Library | 2–3 named patterns, each backed by 3+ stories from *different contexts* |
| **3. Test, Then Earn Your Principles** | Experiments, then sentences | Your first principles + Question Library | 2–3 principles, each traceable through a pattern to real stories |
| **4. From Principles to Practice** | Repeatable moves + your own language | Your personal Design System + Voice | 3–5 documented moves you've used more than once; a language page of phrases that keep recurring |
| **5. Foundations** | The beliefs underneath everything + light governance | Your Constitution + operating system | No exit gate — this stage doesn't end. It compounds. |

Notice the order. Principles arrive at Stage 3, not Stage 1. The
foundational documents come *last*. That's not a quirk of this path —
that's the method itself, and it's how the original Foundry was
actually built: its Constitution was written only after years of
stories made it obvious.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# The One Rule (it governs every stage)

> **Nothing becomes a principle until multiple stories from different
> contexts support it.**

*Different contexts* is the load-bearing phrase. Three stories from the
same job prove something about that job. The same lesson appearing at
work, in your community, and at home is telling you something about how
the world works — and that's what earns its way up.

# What's in This Folder

-   **`stage-1-feed-the-system.md`** through
    **`stage-5-foundations.md`** — one guide per stage. Open only the
    stage you're in. Reading ahead is allowed but rarely useful; the
    later stages only make sense once you're holding the evidence they
    ask about.
-   **`templates.md`** — every structure you'll need, in one place:
    story cards, pattern entries, experiment records, principle
    entries, review rituals. The shapes are borrowed. The content
    never is.
-   **`ai-thinking-partner.md`** — optional. Copy-paste prompts for
    using any AI assistant as a pattern-checking partner at each
    stage — with the guardrails that keep your Foundry *yours*.
-   **`framework.md`**, **`participant-worksheet.md`**, and
    **`worked-example.md`** — the workshop materials this path
    continues from: the Minimum Viable Foundry model, the worksheet
    that builds it, and a real example of one principle being earned
    over twenty years. If you attended the live session, these are
    your reference copies. If you didn't, they're your starting
    point — see below.

# If You Weren't in the Room

You can start this path without the live workshop. Before Stage 1:

1.  Read `framework.md` — the Minimum Viable Foundry, about ten
    minutes.
2.  Work through `participant-worksheet.md` on your own: write 2–3
    story cards, one pattern hunch, one draft question, and an Open
    Questions page with three entries. Give it an honest hour.
3.  Read `worked-example.md` to watch one real principle get earned
    over twenty years — so you know what you're building toward.

Then come back here and begin.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# A Note on Tools (still deliberately last)

The original Foundry lives in a GitHub repository. Yours doesn't have
to. A notebook, a folder of documents, a notes app — anything you'll
actually open every week works. Around Stage 3 or 4 you may want one
file per library instead of one page for everything; let that need
arrive before you solve it. The system is the discipline, not the
software.
