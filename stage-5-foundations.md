# Stage 5 — Foundations

*The Solo Path · typically year one and beyond — and the only stage
with no exit gate*

> Remember what the workshop told you not to do on day one?
>
> Now you're allowed. Because now it won't be a wish list — it'll be a
> description of what a year of evidence already proved.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# You're Here If

You passed the Stage 4 gate — and, just as important, you've felt the
system getting big enough to forget itself: you can't always remember
why you decided something, or where an idea lives.

That feeling is not a failure. It's the exact signal the original
Foundry used to know it needed governance.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# What This Stage Is Really For

Three builds, in this order:

1.  **Your Constitution** — the few beliefs everything else assumes.
2.  **Light governance** — just enough record-keeping that your system
    stays coherent as it grows.
3.  **Public work** — the point of all of it: your Foundry starts
    generating things for other people.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# The Work

## Write the Constitution (finally, and briefly)

Reread everything. Then ask, of each principle and pattern:

> Would my system still be *mine* without this?

The handful of beliefs where the answer is no — the ones every story,
move, and principle quietly assumes — those are your Constitution.
Expect it to be short. The original Foundry's fits on a few pages, and
it was written *last*, after years, precisely so it would be a
foundation instead of a wish list.

Two properties to protect:

-   It should **change more slowly than anything else** you've written.
-   Every line should be **traceable down** through principles and
    patterns to stories. If a line can't be traced, it hasn't been
    earned yet — park it on the Open Questions page.

## Add governance only as it becomes necessary

You already run the only essential piece: the Open Questions page.
Add the rest one at a time, *when the pain appears*, not before:

-   **A decision log** — when you catch yourself re-litigating old
    decisions. One entry per decision: what you decided, why, what
    evidence. (`templates.md` has the shape.)
-   **Your own promotion criteria** — when the one rule needs
    elaborating. Write down your stages and what evidence each
    requires. You've been living them; now record them.
-   **A map** — when you have enough pages that you (or an AI
    assistant working with you) need directions: what lives where, and
    where a new idea belongs.
-   **A quarterly review** — the monthly check's bigger sibling: which
    questions resolved themselves, which ideas deserve promotion,
    which deserve demotion, what's missing.

Governance you add before you need it is bureaucracy. Governance you
add when the system starts forgetting itself is stewardship.

## Generate public work

Your Foundry is now a working system: evidence at the bottom, earned
principles in the middle, tested moves and your own language on top.
Everything you make for the world — talks, workshops, articles, a
coaching practice, an AI assistant that thinks with your system —
should be *generated from* it, and every claim in your public work
should trace back to something in the pile.

That's what changes about your public work from now on: it stops being
opinion and starts being evidence with a voice.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Watch For

-   **The finished-Constitution illusion.** It's a living document
    with a slow pulse, not a monument. When strong new evidence
    contradicts a line, the line changes. Demotion is refinement, not
    failure.
-   **Governance for its own sake.** If a document doesn't answer a
    real recurring pain, retire it. The system should get *simpler* as
    it matures, not heavier.
-   **Public work that drifts.** The further your public work gets
    from your evidence, the more it starts sounding like everyone
    else's. When a talk or post feels generic, the fix is always the
    same: go back to the pile and find the story.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# How the Original Foundry Did This

Foundry's governance suite was created in exactly this order and for
exactly this reason — its own Decision Log records that governance
arrived when "the repository had grown beyond what could be managed
through memory alone." And its Constitution holds itself to the
standard offered here: it changes more slowly than any other document,
and every principle in it was written after the evidence, never
before.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# There Is No Gate at the End

The gate was at the beginning — you needed a year of evidence to stand
here honestly. From now on the path is a rhythm, not a staircase:

-   **Weekly:** one story. Still. Always.
-   **Monthly:** the pattern check.
-   **Quarterly:** the review — promote, demote, retire, notice.
-   **Rarely:** touch the Constitution.

One story a week is fifty stories a year, every year, compounding
under a system that keeps them honest. Most experts retire without
writing down ten.

You're not finishing a Foundry. You're keeping one.

> The goal is not to build a perfect philosophy. The goal is to create
> better conditions for your own thinking, learning, contribution, and
> growth — and now you have a system that does exactly that.
