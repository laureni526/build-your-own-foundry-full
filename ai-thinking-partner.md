# Using AI as Your Foundry's Thinking Partner

*The Solo Path · optional companion — works with any AI assistant*

> AI can help you *see* your evidence. It must never *replace* your
> evidence.
>
> You write the stories. You make the promotion decisions. The AI's
> job is to hold up a mirror — and, just as often, to hold the line
> when your enthusiasm wants to promote something early.

These prompts work in any capable AI assistant (Claude, ChatGPT, or
whatever comes next). Capability travels; tools don't. Copy, paste,
and adapt the wording to sound like you.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# The Guardrails (read these first)

1.  **AI never writes your stories.** It can interview you; the words
    that go on the card are yours. An AI-polished story is evidence
    with fingerprints wiped off it.
2.  **AI never makes promotion decisions.** It can audit your evidence
    and argue either side. The decision — promote or keep observing —
    is yours alone.
3.  **When the AI's version sounds better but doesn't sound like you,
    keep yours.** Better-sounding is how generic creeps in. Your
    Foundry's value is that no one else could have written it.
4.  **AI enthusiasm is not evidence.** Assistants are agreeable by
    default. Every prompt below deliberately instructs the AI to be
    skeptical — keep those instructions in when you adapt them.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Stage 1 — The Story Interviewer (weekly, 10 minutes)

For weeks when the page is blank. The AI asks; you answer; you write
the card yourself.

```
You are helping me capture one story from my week for my personal
knowledge system. Interview me with one question at a time, in this
order: (1) What happened? Push me for specifics — names, stakes,
concrete details — if I'm vague. (2) What did it reveal — what's the
transferable lesson? (3) Where else have I seen this, even as a hunch?

Do not embellish, summarize, or rewrite my story. Do not tell me it's
a great story. When we're done, just show me my own answers organized
under those three headings, in my words, so I can copy them onto my
story card.
```

# Stage 1–2 — The Pattern Check Partner (monthly, 30 minutes)

```
Here are all the story cards in my personal knowledge system.
[paste your stories]

Help me run an honest pattern check:
1. What themes appear in MORE THAN ONE story? List each with the
   stories that support it.
2. For each theme, tell me whether the supporting stories come from
   genuinely DIFFERENT contexts (different jobs, communities, parts of
   life) or from the same context. Be strict — same-context repetition
   is weaker evidence.
3. What am I NOT capturing? Which parts of my life or work appear in
   none of these stories?

Important: your default recommendation is "not yet — keep observing."
Only suggest something might be a real pattern if 3+ stories from
different contexts support it. Do not flatter my stories.
```

# Stage 2–3 — The Promotion Gatekeeper (before any promotion)

Use this every time you're about to promote — hunch to pattern,
pattern to principle. The AI argues *against* you.

```
I'm considering promoting this idea in my personal knowledge system:

"[the hunch or pattern, in one sentence]"

Here is my evidence: [paste the supporting stories/experiments]

My system has one rule: nothing becomes a principle until multiple
stories from DIFFERENT contexts support it.

Your job is to argue AGAINST promotion. Specifically:
1. Is this evidence from genuinely different contexts, or one context
   wearing different outfits?
2. What alternative explanation fits these same stories?
3. Is this idea actually mine, or a common idea from books/culture
   that I may have imported rather than observed?
4. What ONE piece of missing evidence would make this promotion solid?

Then give your verdict: "promote" or "not yet — keep observing," with
one sentence of reasoning. Be strict. I will make the final decision.
```

# Stage 3 — The Experiment Designer

```
I've noticed this pattern in my work: "[pattern]"

Help me design ONE small experiment to deliberately test it. The
constraints: it must fit inside my normal work in the next two weeks,
need no budget or permission, and be small enough that starting is
easier than avoiding it. Frame it so that ANY outcome teaches me
something — the question is "what will we learn?", not "will it work?"

Give me: what to try, what I'd expect if the pattern is real, and what
result would count as evidence AGAINST the pattern.
```

# Stage 4 — The Voice Miner

```
Below is everything I've written in my personal knowledge system —
stories, patterns, principles, experiment notes.
[paste]

Act as an archaeologist of my language. Find:
1. Phrases I've used MORE THAN ONCE without apparently noticing.
2. Metaphors that keep returning.
3. Words I reach for that most people wouldn't choose.

List each with where it appeared. Only include what actually recurs —
do not invent taglines for me, do not improve my phrasing, and do not
suggest new phrases. If something appears only once, leave it out.
```

*Once the Voice Miner has fed a real Language Page — phrases that have
genuinely recurred — there's a next step available: turning that page
into standing instructions so the AI drafts in your voice by default.
That's its own discipline with its own guardrails; see
[`expansions/teach-the-ai-your-voice.md`](expansions/teach-the-ai-your-voice.md).*

# Stage 5 — The Constitution Stress-Tester

```
Here is the draft constitution of my personal knowledge system — the
few beliefs everything else is supposed to rest on:
[paste]

And here are my principles, patterns, and a sample of my stories:
[paste]

For each line of the constitution, test:
1. Can it be traced DOWN through my principles and patterns to actual
   stories? Show the trace or name the gap.
2. Would my system still be recognizably mine without it? (If yes, it
   may not be foundational.)
3. Is it a description of what my evidence shows, or an aspiration
   about who I'd like to be? Flag any aspirations.

Recommend which lines are earned, which should move to my Open
Questions page to earn their place, and which duplicate each other.
I decide what stays.
```

# One Habit That Makes All of This Safer

Whenever an AI session genuinely changes your thinking, capture that
as a story card too — what happened, what it revealed, where else
you've seen it. Your AI conversations are part of your lived evidence
now. Treat them with the same discipline as everything else: observed,
recorded, and never promoted on enthusiasm alone.
