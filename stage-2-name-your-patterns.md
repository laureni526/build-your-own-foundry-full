# Stage 2 — Name Your Patterns

*The Solo Path · typically months 1–3 of the journey, but the gate
decides, not the calendar*

> A hunch becomes a pattern the day the evidence says so — and the day
> you give it a name you'd actually say out loud.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# You're Here If

You passed the Stage 1 gate: 10–15 stories from more than one part of
your life, at least three honest pattern checks, and a hunch that's
been tested by new evidence.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# What This Stage Is Really For

Until now, everything you've noticed has lived on your Open Questions
page, labeled as unproven. This stage is the first promotion your
system performs: moving a hunch that has *earned it* into a new home —
your Pattern Library.

This matters more than it looks. A named pattern is the first piece of
your expertise that exists outside a specific story. It's the first
thing you can teach.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# The Work

## Promote what the evidence supports

For each hunch on your Open Questions page, ask the promotion
questions:

-   Does it appear in **three or more stories**?
-   Do those stories come from **different contexts** — different jobs,
    different parts of life, different people?
-   Is it a pattern, or a coincidence with good marketing?

If yes to all three: promote it. Start a Pattern Library page and give
the pattern an entry (`templates.md` has the shape): a name, a
one-sentence claim, the list of stories that support it, and — this
part keeps you honest — a line for counter-evidence.

If yes to only some: it stays a hunch. Write down exactly what evidence
is missing, so your weekly stories know what to watch for.

## Name it in your own words

The name matters. Not a book's name for it, not the facilitator's name
for it — the words *you'd* use telling a friend. If your pattern is
real, you've probably been saying it informally for years. Listen for
that phrasing; it's already yours.

## Start collecting your questions

Somewhere in this stage you'll notice something else repeating: the
*questions you keep asking other people* — the ones that reliably
unlock someone's thinking. Start a simple list on your Open Questions
page. It becomes its own library in Stage 3.

## Keep feeding the system

The weekly story and monthly check don't stop — not in this stage, not
in any stage. New stories are what test the patterns you just named.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Watch For

-   **Promotion by enthusiasm.** The pattern you're most excited about
    and the pattern with the best evidence are often not the same one.
    Promote the second.
-   **Borrowed patterns.** If your pattern could be a chapter title in
    a business book, check whether you found it in your stories or
    imported it from your reading. Borrowed patterns aren't wrong —
    they're just not *yours*, and this system only compounds on what's
    yours.
-   **Pattern sprawl.** Two or three well-evidenced patterns beat ten
    thin ones. If everything is a pattern, nothing is.
-   **Same-context evidence.** Five stories from one job feel like
    proof. They're proof about that job. Hold the line.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# How the Original Foundry Did This

One of Foundry's named patterns is *"Waiting for Permission"* — people
don't start, not because they can't, but because they aren't sure
they're allowed. It began as a repeated observation across unrelated
settings, sat as a hunch, and was only named once stories from
different contexts kept confirming it. Later it seeded an experiment,
then a practice, then a framework that anchored a keynote. Every one of
those began with the naming you're doing now.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# The Gate — You've Earned Stage 3 When

-   [ ] You have **2–3 named patterns** in a Pattern Library of your
    own.
-   [ ] Each is backed by **3+ stories from different contexts**,
    listed in the entry.
-   [ ] Each entry has an honest **counter-evidence line** — even if
    it currently says "none yet."
-   [ ] Your weekly capture habit is **still alive** (gaps included).

When all four are true, open `stage-3-earn-your-principles.md`.
