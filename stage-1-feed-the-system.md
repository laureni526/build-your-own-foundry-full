# Stage 1 — Feed the System

*The Solo Path · typically the first 1–3 months, but the gate decides,
not the calendar*

> One story a week. One honest review a month. That's the whole stage.
>
> It's also the stage everything else depends on.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# You're Here If

You have your Minimum Viable Foundry from the workshop (or the
worksheet): one question, a few story cards, one hunch labeled as a
hunch, an Open Questions page, and a chosen writing day.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# What This Stage Is Really For

Right now your Foundry is a promise. This stage turns it into a body
of evidence.

Nothing gets promoted here. Nothing gets named, organized, or declared
true. You are doing one thing: building the pile of stories that every
later stage will draw from. The experts whose frameworks die young
skipped this stage. Don't.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# The Work

## The weekly story (10 minutes)

On your writing day, capture one story using the card structure
(`templates.md` has it):

-   **What happened** — the concrete event. Names, stakes, specifics.
-   **What it revealed** — the transferable lesson.
-   **Where else I've seen it** — even a hunch.

What makes a story worth capturing? Not that it's impressive — that
it's *specific*. A five-minute automation someone built without asking
permission teaches more than an award. And capture the flops. In the
original Foundry, the single most load-bearing story is a failure: a
tool that technically worked and nobody used. Failures are evidence
too — often the best kind, because nobody polishes them.

If you're stuck on your writing day, use the workshop's prompts:

-   A moment this week that surprised you — that you keep thinking about
-   A time you were sure you were right, and weren't
-   Something that worked, that you've never explained to anyone
-   Advice you gave this week — and the moment that originally taught it to you

## The monthly pattern check (30 minutes)

Once a month, reread *everything* you've captured and ask three
questions:

1.  **What keeps repeating?** Note it on your Open Questions page as a
    hunch — don't promote it yet.
2.  **How is my original hunch doing?** Did new stories from *different
    contexts* confirm it? Did something contradict it? Update its entry
    honestly.
3.  **Does my question still fit?** If your best new stories keep
    escaping your North Star question, the question is too small.
    Widen it — a question that changes is a question that's working.

That's the entire ritual. `templates.md` has a checklist version.

# Watch For (the ways this stage quietly fails)

-   **The missed week.** You will miss weeks. A missed week is a data
    point, not a verdict — write two cards the next week or just
    resume. The system survives gaps. It doesn't survive quitting.
-   **Polishing.** Story cards are evidence, not essays. If a card
    takes more than fifteen minutes, you're writing for an audience
    that doesn't exist yet.
-   **Only capturing wins.** A story library with no failures in it is
    a résumé, and résumés don't reveal patterns.
-   **Tool migration.** Somewhere around week three you'll be tempted
    to move everything to a better app. That urge is the work avoiding
    you. Write this week's story first; move tools after, if you still
    want to.
-   **Early promotion.** You'll feel certain about your hunch by story
    six. Certainty isn't the bar — evidence from different contexts is.
    Park the certainty on your Open Questions page and keep going.

# How the Original Foundry Did This

Foundry's Story Library grew for years before a single principle was
written. Its Repository History records that the Constitution and
Working Canon were written only after the stories made a philosophy
impossible to ignore. You are not behind. You are exactly where the
worked example started.

# The Gate — You've Earned Stage 2 When

-   [ ] You have **10–15 story cards** (more is fine; fewer means keep
    going).
-   [ ] They come from **more than one part of your life** — not all
    from the same job or project.
-   [ ] You've done **at least three monthly pattern checks**.
-   [ ] Your original hunch has been **confirmed by new evidence,
    refuted, or replaced** — and all three of those count as success,
    because each one means the system is doing its job.

When all four are true, open `stage-2-name-your-patterns.md`.

If they're not true yet: not yet. Keep observing. That's not a delay —
that *is* the method.
