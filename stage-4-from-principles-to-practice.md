# Stage 4 — From Principles to Practice

*The Solo Path · typically months 6–12 of the journey, but the gate
decides, not the calendar*

> A principle tells you what's true. A practice tells you what to do
> at 2pm on a Tuesday when you see it happening.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# You're Here If

You passed the Stage 3 gate: 2–3 earned principles with full evidence
trails, at least two recorded experiments, and a Question Library of
your own.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# What This Stage Is Really For

Your system now knows things. This stage makes it *do* things — and
starts capturing how it sounds.

Two builds happen here:

1.  **Your moves** — repeatable interventions in the form *"when I see
    X, I do Y"*, each anchored to a principle. This becomes your
    personal design system.
2.  **Your language** — the phrases and metaphors you keep reaching
    for, mined from everything you've written so far. This becomes
    your voice page.

Together they're what make your expertise *transferable* — the point
where someone else could pick up a page of yours and use it.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# The Work

## Document your moves

Reread your experiment records and your recent stories, looking for
interventions you've now used **more than once**, on purpose, with
useful results. Write each one as a move (`templates.md` has the
shape):

-   **When I see...** — the trigger, concretely.
-   **I do...** — the intervention, concretely enough that someone
    else could try it.
-   **Because...** — the principle it rests on.
-   **Evidence** — where it's worked, where it's failed.

Aim for three to five. A move must survive the same discipline as
everything else: used more than once, in more than one situation,
anchored to a principle you've earned. One clever thing you did once
is a story, not a move.

## Mine your language

Reread your entire Foundry — every story, pattern, principle, and
experiment. You're listening for repetition of a different kind now:

-   Phrases you've written more than once without noticing
-   Metaphors that keep returning
-   The way you say your principles out loud when teaching someone,
    which is often better than the way you wrote them

Start a language page. The rule carries over exactly: a phrase earns a
spot only when it recurs naturally across real work. You cannot write
your voice in an afternoon — but by now, you don't have to. It's
already in the pile. You're just collecting it.

## Keep feeding the system

Weekly story. Monthly check. Still the engine. By now the monthly
check has more to review — stories, patterns, experiments, moves — so
let it grow to 45 minutes if it needs to.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Watch For

-   **Inventing taglines.** The voice work is *archaeology*, not
    branding. If a phrase sounds great but has never appeared in your
    actual work, it goes on the Open Questions page to earn its place,
    not on the language page.
-   **Tool-shaped moves.** If a move only works inside one specific
    app or platform, it's an implementation, not a move. Rewrite it at
    the level that survives the tool disappearing.
-   **Moves without principles.** A move you can't trace to a
    principle is a habit. Maybe a good one — but park it until you
    know *why* it works.
-   **Skipping the reread.** The full reread is the whole method here.
    Your voice and your moves are found in the pile, not composed at
    the keyboard.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# How the Original Foundry Did This

Foundry's Design System is a set of named moves like *Lower the
Activation Energy* and *Design for Self-Permission* — each one anchored
to canon principles and backed by stories. If you attended the live
workshop, you watched them operate: the session itself was built from
them, deliberately. And Foundry's Voice System — its signature phrases
and metaphors — was assembled the same way you're about to: by
noticing which language kept recurring across real work, and admitting
only what recurred.

# The Gate — You've Earned Stage 5 When

-   [ ] You have **3–5 documented moves**, each used more than once,
    each traceable to a principle.
-   [ ] You have a **language page** where every entry has appeared at
    least twice in your real work, unprompted.
-   [ ] **Someone else could run one of your moves** from what you
    wrote — the real test: hand one page to a trusted colleague and
    ask them to try it.
-   [ ] You've started to feel the size: you occasionally **can't
    remember why you decided something**. (That feeling is the
    entrance ticket to Stage 5.)

When all four are true, open `stage-5-foundations.md`.
